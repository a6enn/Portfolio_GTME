Builder. Operator.

I've never been the person in the room who says "someone should build that." I'm the person who goes home and builds it.

That instinct showed up early. Consulting gave me the foundation — financial modeling at Deloitte, digital transformation at Capco, a $200M account turnaround at Cognizant. I learned how to diagnose complex problems, communicate at the executive level, and operate inside organizations where the stakes are real. But I was always more interested in what happened after the diagnosis. Who was going to fix it? How?

The answer got sharper when I joined Arteria as the first NYC GTM hire. No inherited relationships, no motion, no playbook. Just a mandate to build pipeline at a company most of the market had never heard of. I didn't wait for someone to hand me a system. I built one — pulling tools together, engineering sequences, testing and iterating until it worked. Somewhere in that process I realized I'd stumbled into a new kind of skill: GTM engineering. I didn't know it had a name. I just knew I needed to solve the problem.

That's the through-line. Every role, every company, every problem — I find the gap and build the thing. The prospecting workflow on this page isn't a case study. It's live, and it's what runs my territory. I'm not learning to work with AI. I built around it.
