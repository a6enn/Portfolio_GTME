The Problem

Manual prospecting doesn't scale.

As an AE at Arteria AI, I was responsible for new logo growth in financial services. The ICP is narrow — global banks, broker-dealers, hedge funds, and asset managers with specific document workflows — and the wrong outreach burns credibility fast in a small industry.

Doing this right the manual way meant: researching each firm, scoring it against the ICP, finding the right contacts, personalizing the message to the firm's specific situation. For one firm, that's an hour. For a pipeline, it's weeks.

I needed to do the work of a research team with just myself and the right tools. So I built a system.

The System

A five-step automated pipeline.

The workflow takes a CSV of firm names as input and produces fully-written, personalized outreach emails as output — with a qualification gate in the middle that skips firms that don't meet the bar.

The Frameworks

Codified GTM knowledge, not just automation.

The system is only as good as what you put into it. What made this work wasn't the code — it was encoding real GTM judgment into the prompts.

ICP Scoring — 25-point framework across 5 dimensions:

# Each firm is scored 1–5 on: Workflow Replacement Potential # Critical gate — score of 1–2 = auto-skip Document Complexity # Complexity of agreement types they handle Downstream Data Need # How much structured data they need from docs Strategic Timing # Regulatory triggers, hiring signals, fund activity Buyer Access # Likelihood of reaching the right person # Score thresholds: 20–25 → Priority 14–19 → Strong fit <15 → Auto-skip (no outreach generated)

Email structure — four-beat framework:

Beat 1: Context # Firm-specific observation — shows you did the work Beat 2: Insight # Make them feel seen — the documentation problem they live with Beat 3: Positioning # One outcome, no features, no product name in first email Beat 4: Close # Low-friction ask — 15 minutes, not "a quick call" # Hard constraints: Body: 100–150 words (hard cap) Subject: under 8 words, operational tension, no buzzwords Banned: em dashes, "quick question", "following up", "partnership"

These aren't arbitrary rules. They're distilled from what actually works in regulated financial services — where decision-makers are sophisticated, time-poor, and allergic to vendor-speak.

What This Demonstrates

GTM thinking + AI execution.

Before this system, researching and writing outreach for a single firm took 2–3 hours — and the output was still less thorough than what this produces automatically. That's the real outcome: not just speed, but the ability to run a research-quality prospecting operation solo, at a pace that would otherwise require a team.

The ICP framework came from real deal cycles. The messaging rules came from real email tests. If you understand your market deeply enough to write down the rules, you can automate the work that follows from those rules. The code is almost beside the point — and this workflow is how I run my territory now.
